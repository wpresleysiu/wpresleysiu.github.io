Burgers

    Ingredients

  • Ground Beef

    About 1/3 lbs of beef per burger.

  • Cheese

  • Buns

  • Toppings

    Ketchup, mustard, tomatoes, avacado, eggs, bacon, marshmallows,
    ...anything you want!

Start with a pound of ground beef, and divide it up into three parts. A third of a pound will make a nice big, juicy burger. 80/20 ground chuck is the best option for burgers. The high fat content will help to keep your burgers from getting too dry. Some people like to buy lean ground beef because it's healthier, but if the extra 10% fat content is going to destroy your diet, maybe you should just skip the burger altogether. A grilled chicken sandwich is also delicious and might be a better option than a dry burger.

Handle your beef delicately (I'm sorry, I couldn't think of a better way to say that). Try to form your patties as loosely as you can into about one inch thick discs. Then, press an indentation into the middle of each patty. You can apply your favorite seasoning, or just use salt, pepper, and garlic.
